Spaghetti Bolognese

Beef stew served with bread

Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian-inspired pasta dish featuring a rich, hearty meat sauce served over spaghetti. The sauce is made with ground meat (usually beef), tomatoes, and a mix of vegetables and herbs, cooked slowly to bring out a deep, savory flavor. It's a staple in many households and is often regarded as a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery sticks, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato purée
  • Beef stock (approx. 150ml)
  • Dried oregano and bay leaf
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spaghetti

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Cook for about 5–7 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for one more minute until fragrant.
  4. Increase the heat and add the ground beef. Cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
  5. Stir in the tomato purée, chopped tomatoes, beef stock, and herbs. Bring the sauce to a boil.
  6. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for at least 15–20 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  7. While the sauce simmers, cook the spaghetti in a separate pot of boiling, salted water according to the packet instructions.
  8. Drain the spaghetti and serve with the bolognese sauce on top.
