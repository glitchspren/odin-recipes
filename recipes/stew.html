Stew

Beef stew served with bread

Beef stew is a warm, thick soup made with tender pieces of beef and soft vegetables like carrots and potatoes. The meat and vegetables are cooked slowly in a rich, savory gravy. It's a classic comfort food, especially for a cold day.

Bread is a perfect partner for beef stew, often used to soak up every last bit of the tasty, thick sauce. You can use a crusty loaf to dip into the stew or a softer roll to enjoy alongside it. The bread adds a great contrast in texture and completes the satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • Beef: Chuck roast or other stew meat.
  • Vegetables: Carrots, potatoes, celery, and onions.
  • Liquid: Beef broth, and sometimes red wine or water.
  • Thickening agents: All-purpose flour and tomato paste.
  • Seasonings: Garlic, bay leaves, thyme, salt, pepper, and Worcestershire sauce.
  • Oil: Olive or vegetable oil for browning the meat and sautéing the vegetables.

Steps

  1. Prepare the beef: Cut chuck roast or other stew meat into 1-inch cubes. Season the beef generously with salt and pepper, then toss with a light coating of all-purpose flour.
  2. Sear the beef: Heat oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the beef in batches until browned on all sides, then remove to a plate.
  3. Sauté the vegetables: Add chopped onions, carrots, and celery to the pot. Cook until the onions have softened, about 5 to 7 minutes. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Deglaze the pot: Pour in some red wine (optional) or beef broth to scrape up the browned bits from the bottom of the pot. This adds a lot of flavor.
  5. Add liquid and simmer: Return the beef to the pot, along with beef broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, and herbs like bay leaves and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a gentle simmer.
  6. Slow cook: Cover the pot and let the stew simmer for 1 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  7. Add potatoes: Stir in the cubed potatoes and continue to simmer until the potatoes are tender, about 15 to 30 minutes.
  8. Thicken and serve: For a thicker gravy, mix a little cornstarch with cold water and stir it into the simmering stew. Cook for a few more minutes until thickened. Remove the bay leaf before serving.
